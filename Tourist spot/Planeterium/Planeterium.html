Kalpana Chawla Memorial Planetarium

Astronomy has its extensive elaboration in Indian ancient Vedic scriptures. It has its relation with Indian civilization dating back to pre historic times. This relation is thoughtfully developed as Kalpana Chawla Memorial Planetarium in Kurukshetra. This planetarium situated on Pehowa Road of Kurukshetra was built in fond memory of Kalpana Chawla and named after her. It was constructed jointly by Haryana State Council for Science & Technology and National Council of Science Museums (NCSM) under Ministry of Culture, Govt. of India.

The total cost of its construction that amounted to approx. 6.5 crores was shared by state and central government in equal proportion. This sprawling planetarium developed on 5 acres of land was built and dedicated to nation in July 2007 by Honorable Chief Minister of Haryana, Shri Bhupinder Singh Hooda.

The planetarium is surrounded by plush green areas and it has been developed as an interesting tourist place. The outer area of planetarium called as Astro Park and its inside has many programmes and exhibits that offer comprehensive information on universe and astronomical sciences.

This sophisticated planetarium has special Splitz Display system, opto mechanical projection along with digital dome video display. This unique equipment placed inside 12 meter dome of planetarium takes you thousands of year earlier or later in history to understand the vision of night sky in any period.
